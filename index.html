Highlighting Black Queer activists through history

Check out our featured blog posts

Marsha P Johnson

Marsha P. Johnson was one of the most prominent figures of the gay rights movement of the 1960s and 1970s in New York City.

Audre Lorde

A self-described “black, lesbian, mother, warrior, poet,” Audre Lorde dedicated both her life and her creative talent to confronting and addressing injustices of racism, sexism, classism, and homophobia.

James Baldwin

A writer and civil rights activist who is best known for his semi-autobiographical novels and plays that center on race, politics, and sexuality.
